How Many People in the UK Are Financially Comfortable

We wanted to investigate how many people are financially comfortable in the UK. To do this, we looked at median salary data for 147 administrative areas from the Office of National Statistics and cost of living data from livingcost.org to find out what percentage of people in the UK can afford to live by the 50-30-20 rule for budgeting.

What is the 50-30-20 Rule?

The 50-30-20 rule suggests spending:

  • 50% of post-tax earnings on essentials (rent, utilities, food).
  • 30% on wants (weekend trips, entertainment, etc.).
  • 20% into savings.

Key Findings

  • The average take-home salary a single person needs to live comfortably in the UK is £36,239 (pre-tax: £46,000).

  • The average post-tax household income needed for a family of four to live comfortably is £88,143 (pre-tax: £147,000).

  • 41% of single people can afford to “live comfortably” in the UK.

  • 27% of families can afford to “live comfortably” in the UK.

Methodology

To find out how much it costs to live comfortably in each administrative region, we looked at cost of living data from livingcost.org. This provided essential costs for each area, and we extrapolated this using the 50-30-20 budgeting principle to work out how much was needed to live comfortably.

We calculated how many people could live comfortably in each area using the 2024 Office of National Statistics report on earnings and hours worked by local authority.
